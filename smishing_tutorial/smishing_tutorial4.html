Vishing Tutorial

Learn Vishing and how to detect them in few easy steps.

How to protect yourself from vishing attacks:

1. Register your phone-number to National Do-Not-Call Registry: It is free to add your home or cell phone to this list and inform telemarketers you don’t want their telephone calls. Here is their web-site https://www.donotcall.gov/

  a. Note however that it will not completely prevent people from illegally reaching your number.

2. Do not answer calls from unknown numbers: Use caller-ID wherever possible. Note though that caller IDs may be faked, meaning that even if the number looks local, it actually may not be. Simply, do not answer if you do not recognize the number. Just let them communicate to voicemail. That will give you time to understand and decide whether to call back after verifying the caller.

3. Look for red-flags: If you do answer an unknown call, look for red flags such as someone trying to raise urgency or asking to send you money or revel any sensitive information.

  a. Never give anyone your social security or other personal, sensitive information. If a legitimate company need to verify your social security, they will ONLY ask for last 4 digits, never the full number.

  b. Never send money when an unknown caller calls you, regardless of the situation. You should take down the information and then verify if the caller is legitimate.

4. Hang up: As soon as you suspect that it is a scam phone call, don’t feel compelled to carry on a polite conversation. Just hang up and block that number.
